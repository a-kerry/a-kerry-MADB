Future Implications of AI tools


This page aims to discuss the various future scenarios and implications between AI and video content creation.
Such as: Monopolisation of content creation and a 'Human-Made' premium.

The Harvard Business Review 'How Generative AI Could Disrupt Creative Work' by David De Cremer, Nicola Morini Bianzino, and Ben Falk, presents multiple possible scenarios surrounding the topic:

Monopolization of Creativity by Machines

In this scenario, algorithmically generated content floods the market, overshadowing human creators. Lawsuits over copyright infringement highlight the inadequacy of intellectual property laws in addressing AI-generated content. If left unchecked, this could lead to a decline in authentic human creativity, slower innovation, and increased competition for attention. Personalized content may exacerbate societal divisions and filter bubbles. Curation becomes more valuable, favouring established artists over newcomers.

"Human-Made" Premium

Alternatively, a backlash against algorithmically generated content may emerge, leading people to value authentic human creativity more highly. Humans retain an advantage in producing accurate, culturally relevant content that adapts to rapid cultural changes. Political leadership may be necessary to address the risks associated with misinformation and content moderation.

In summary, the future of creativity could be shaped by a struggle between algorithmic efficiency and human authenticity, with potential consequences for innovation, societal cohesion, and governance.

Advancement of Tools – Chat GPT ‘SORA’


Furthermore, there will be a continuation of advancement of AI tools, for example, the unveiling of ‘SORA’ from OpenAI, the creator of Chat GPT. Sora is an AI model that can create realistic and imaginative scenes from text instructions. We’re teaching AI to understand and simulate the physical world in motion, with the goal of training models that help people solve problems that require real-world interaction. Introducing Sora, our text-to-video model. Sora can generate videos up to a minute long while maintaining visual quality and adherence to the user’s prompt.
