Disadvantages of AI tools


This Page aims to discuss and present the disadvantages of AI tools for creative video projects

Overview of the Disadvantages of AI tools for creative video projects


While AI offers new possibilities for creative expression, it also poses challenges to the unique qualities and personal experiences that human creators bring to their work. Where there is the possibility of related disadvantages such as the concerns moderations and governance of AI tools.



Dehumanisation of creativity:

It is proposed that AI tools can dehumanise creativity by challenging traditional notions of human agency, expression, emotion, and labour involved in regular creative processes.

How AI lacks a basic understanding of human values, socially and emotional:

Additionally, as AI lacks human experience and emotions that drive creative work, this can potentially lessen the role of human’s creators, raising questions over the authenticity of AI-generated projects.
Relative to content creators who employ AI tools for their video projects, this could result in the audience questioning the creator’s authenticity, affecting viewer creator relationships.

Perspective of Viewers:

With content creators adopting AI tools, this has the possibility to jepordise the realtionship with fans and viewers:
A case Example is that of Youtuber, Jordi van den Bussche, known as 'Kwebbelkop'. A gaming youtuber who adopted various AI tools, creating a 'clone' version of himself creating content without him in it.
As seen here at an AI event:

For a period of time the Youtuber did use the AI tools created however due to backlash and negative feedback from audiences, decided not to continue with the 'Clone'.
A take on this topic comes from Lia Haberman, an influencer marketing expert and instructor at UCLA. Who stated, “This presents an entirely new option for creators to essentially clone themselves and continue without worrying about aging, gaining weight, or otherwise evolving in any way that could alienate certain segments of their audience,”.
But however is not fully convinced by these content types, stating Their appeal “is their humanity and ability to create these parasocial relationships with their audience where people either relate to them or aspire to become them,” she says. “A virtual influencer will only ever present as entertainment, at least until we get to sentient beings.”
